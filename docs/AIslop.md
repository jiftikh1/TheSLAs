1. Define “AI Slop” Explicitly

AI slop is content that sounds correct but adds no lived insight.

Common signals:

Generic phrasing (“powerful platform”, “robust ecosystem”)

Balanced-but-empty takes

Vague pros/cons with no context

Overly polished, blog-like tone

No evidence of time-in-system

Important:
Not malicious. Just useless.

2. Slop Prevention Philosophy

We do not:

Ban AI

Shame users

Require “prove you’re human”

We do:

Make slop unrewarding

Make lived experience the easiest path

Let Claude quietly enforce quality

3. Pre-Publish Friction (Critical)

Before a post goes live, Claude runs a Slop Risk Assessment.

Slop Risk Signals

High fluency + low specificity

No named workflows, systems, or failure modes

Excessive hedging (“in general”, “it depends”, “overall”)

Symmetry bias (equal pros/cons with no stance)

Claude Interventions (Soft, Not Blocking)

If slop risk is high, Claude nudges:

“This reads a bit high-level.
Can you anchor it to:
– a specific workflow
– a moment where something broke
– a tradeoff you only noticed after time?”

User can:

Edit and improve

Post anyway (but see below)

4. Slop Penalty Without Punishment
If Posted As-Is

The system:

Publishes the content

Assigns low initial trust

Excludes it from:

Trend detection

Summaries

Overtime graphs

Does not lift user reputation

Slop dies naturally. No drama.

5. Anti-AI Pattern Detection (Post-Publish)

Claude continuously evaluates:

Linguistic Slop Patterns

Repeated sentence structures

Marketing-adjacent language

Excessive polish vs peer tone

Semantic Slop Patterns

Says nothing falsifiable

Could apply to any software

No time-based claims

When detected:

Trust score is capped

Reputation lift is suppressed

Content is deprioritized in feeds

6. Lived-Experience Anchors (Positive Incentive)

Claude actively looks for anchors:

“After X months”

“Post-implementation”

“During quarter close”

“Once we crossed Y users / volume”

“The first thing that broke was…”

Anchors:

Boost trust

Boost reputation

Increase visibility

This makes real experience the fastest way to influence the system.

7. AI-Assisted ≠ AI-Generated

Allowed and encouraged:

Users drafting with AI

Cleaning up grammar

Structuring thoughts

Not rewarded:

Raw AI output pasted in

Generic summaries

Rewritten marketing pages

Claude distinguishes:

Human insight + AI polish ✅

AI insight pretending to be human ❌

8. Rate Limits That Feel Human

Soft constraints:

Post frequency dampening

No reputation lift for rapid-fire posts

Trust gains require time separation

This mirrors how real expertise shows up.

9. Community Echo Validation

AI slop rarely gets:

“Matches my experience”

Follow-up stories

Independent confirmation

Claude weights echoed lived experiences heavily.

Slop stays lonely.

10. Slop-Safe Summaries

Claude will not summarize:

Generic sentiment

Low-signal consensus

Instead it surfaces:

Specific disagreements

Edge cases

“This breaks when…”

This prevents AI amplifying AI.

11. Transparent Messaging (Light Touch)

Occasional copy like:

“Specific experiences shape the most trusted insights here.”

No lectures. No warnings. Just norms.

12. How This Integrates With Trust & Reputation
System Component	Slop Impact
Post Trust Score	Capped
Reputation Score	No lift
Trend Graphs	Excluded
Summaries	Ignored
Visibility	Naturally low

Slop is not removed.
It’s simply irrelevant.

13. Why This Works

AI slop relies on visibility

You remove visibility, not permission

Experts don’t feel policed

New users learn by example

The system improves over time

This is how you keep the forum useful five years in, not just at launch.

14. Claude’s Explicit Contract

Claude must:

Prefer specificity over polish

Reward falsifiable claims

Ask for experience, not opinions

Suppress generic consensus

Amplify uncomfortable truths

Claude is the immune system, not the judge.
