PRD: Peer Software Reality Check (Working Title)
1. Vision & Purpose

Create an honest, transparent, practitioner-driven forum for discussing software — how it actually works in the real world.

Think:

Gartner-level breadth

Reddit / Blind-level candor

Without pay-to-play rankings

Without vendor-controlled narratives

This is not about who paid to be in the top-right quadrant.
It’s about what breaks, what scales, and what to watch out for.

2. Core Principles

Practitioner truth > vendor marketing

Anonymity with accountability

Multi-dimensional evaluation (not a single score)

Long-term trends over point-in-time hot takes

No pricing discussions (by design)

3. Target Users & Personas
Primary Personas

Practitioners

Admins, Systems Engineers, RevOps, BizTech, IT

Care about integrations, workflows, edge cases, reliability

Want to warn others and learn from peers

Power Users

ICs who live in the tool daily

Care about usability, performance, limitations

Leaders

Directors, VPs, CIOs

Care about trajectory, risk, long-term viability, ecosystem health

Each persona gets a distinct lens on the same software.

4. Authentication & Identity Model
Login

LinkedIn OAuth required

Ensures real professionals

Prevents spam, bots, and low-effort trash

Posting Identity

Posts are anonymous by default

Internal system still knows:

Role

Industry

Company size (bucketed)

Seniority level

This allows:

Credibility signals without name exposure

Role-based filtering and views

5. Content Structure
Software Profile Pages

Each software product has a living profile broken into dimensions, not a single score.

Dimensions

Partnerships

Vendor relationship quality

Responsiveness

Roadmap trustworthiness

Integrations

Depth vs surface-level

Reliability

Maintenance burden

Workflows

What it’s great at

Where it becomes painful

What breaks at scale

Issues & Limitations

Known landmines

Things you only learn 6–12 months in

“Wish I knew this earlier”

Each dimension can be:

Rated (qualitative scale)

Commented on independently

6. Time-Series & Trajectory Tracking (Key Differentiator)
Overtime Graphs

For each software + dimension:

Show trend over time

Improving 📈

Flat ➖

Declining 📉

This answers:

“Is this product getting better or worse?”

“Did the acquisition/helpful roadmap actually materialize?”

No single static verdict — just momentum and direction.

Repeat contributors

11. Non-Goals (Important)

Not a review site

Not a pricing comparison tool

Not a vendor marketing channel

Not a sales lead gen platform

This is infrastructure for collective wisdom, not hype.

12. Long-Term Vision

Over time, this becomes:

The place operators check before committing

A counterbalance to glossy analyst reports

A living memory of how software behaves at scale
